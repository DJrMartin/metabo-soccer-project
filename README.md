# Maximal Fat Oxidation and High-Intensity Running Performance in Elite Players

## ABSTRACT

**Purpose.** Maximal fat oxidation rate (MFO) is considered an important
determinant of athletic performance in endurance sports, but its impact
on physical performance in team sports, especially soccer, remains
poorly understood. This study examined the possible associations between
MFO and in-game running performance in elite soccer players.

**Methods.** During the pre-season, 41 youth elite soccer players
performed a maximal and a fasted submaximal graded exercise test on a
treadmill to determine peak oxygen uptake (\dot{V}O\_{2max}) and MFO,
respectively. In-game running performances were measured by a global
positioning system (GPS) over a competitive season. We estimate the
linear and nonlinear dependency between physiological variables and
in-game performance.

**Results.** MFO is linear and non-linear, dependent on high-intensity
running (\> 15 km·h-1). MFO greater than 0.73 g·min-1 is associated with
a higher high-intensity running performance during matches (p\<0.01). We
also display a positive synergy between \dot{V}O\_{2max} and MFO that
led to an increase in the total distance during matches.

**Conclusion.** These findings highlight the importance of maximal fat
oxidation capacity to perform moderate to high-intensity efforts during
soccer matches, and suggest that this athletic performance indicator
should be more widely assessed and developed by fitness coaches of elite
professional soccer teams.

**Keywords**: fat oxidation, exercise physiology, football, metabolic
profile, anaerobic threshold, performance

## DATASET

This study utilized two main datasets:

**RAW_DATA.csv** : This dataset contains the non-standardized GPS data
used to observe the physical performance of players during matches. It
also includes all the physical and physiological variables collected
during the study.

**STANDARDIZED_DATA.csv** : This dataset contains the standardized data
used in the study to observe the impact of physiological variables on
physical performance during matches.
